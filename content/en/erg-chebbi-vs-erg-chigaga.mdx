Morocco has two major sand seas worth travelling for: Erg Chebbi near Merzouga (east) and Erg Chigaga near M'Hamid (south). Both are real Saharan desert. Both deliver starry nights, camel rides, and sand dunes. But they're very different experiences — and choosing the wrong one for your trip is a common mistake.

## At a glance

| | Erg Chebbi | Erg Chigaga |
|---|---|---|
| Location | Merzouga (southeast) | M'Hamid (south) |
| Drive from Marrakech | 8–9 hours | 6 hours to Zagora + 1.5h piste |
| Dune height | Up to 150 m | Up to 60 m |
| Size | ~28 km × 7 km | ~40 km × 10 km |
| Road access | Paved to the dune edge | Piste (4x4 required) |
| Solo access | Easy | Needs guided expedition |
| Camp options | Many, all budgets | Few, mostly organised |
| Crowds (high season) | Moderate | Low |
| Minimum days | 3 | 5 |
| Price (3-day trip) | €150–500/person | €250–600/person |

## Erg Chebbi in depth

### The dunes
The most photographed dunes in Morocco for good reason. Erg Chebbi's sand is a distinctive deep orange — a product of its iron-rich composition — and the main dunes rise up to 150 metres. The classic shot (camel caravan at sunset against a wall of orange sand) is real and achievable on any standard trip.

The erg itself is about 28 km long and 7 km wide at its broadest. The eastern edge of the dunes rises directly from a gravel plain; the western edge has the village of Merzouga and most of the camps.

### Getting there
Straightforward. The N9/N10 road is fully paved all the way to Merzouga. A standard rental car handles it without problems. You can drive yourself, take a bus to Erfoud and taxi from there, or book a tour. No guide is required to arrive.

### The camps
The most competitive desert camp market in Morocco. At any given time there are dozens of options from basic (€15–20/person) to luxury glamping with private bathrooms (€100–200/person). If Erg Chebbi has a flaw, it's that **the camps are all on the western edge of the erg** — walk east into the dunes and the crowds dissipate; walk west toward the road and you're back in tourist territory.

### When it's best
Sunrise. Even in a busy camp, the first light on the dunes — that specific moment when the sand goes from grey to gold to orange — is spectacular. And the main camel caravan stops are 25–30 minutes from camp by camel, far enough that sound doesn't carry from the road.

### The honest drawback
In April and October, you'll share the main dunes with other groups. Not constant crowds — the erg is big — but not solitude either. You need to walk 40–60 minutes to escape the camp zone entirely.

## Erg Chigaga in depth

### The dunes
Slightly larger in total area than Erg Chebbi but with lower maximum dune heights. The sand color is more golden-brown, less intensely orange. The overall aesthetic is softer, more subtle. In photographs it's less dramatic; on the ground, it feels more intimate.

### Getting there
From Marrakech: 6 hours to Zagora on paved road, then 2 hours to M'Hamid, then 50 km of piste (unpaved desert track, marked but rough) to reach the dunes. A 4x4 is required for the piste section. Driving the piste independently without local knowledge is not recommended — tracks multiply and divert, and you can end up significantly off course.

Most travellers go with a local operator from M'Hamid who provides the 4x4 and a guide. The logistics are more involved and more expensive than Merzouga.

### The camps
Fewer options and less variety than Merzouga. Most camps at Erg Chigaga are part of organised expeditions and tend toward mid-range or above. There are genuinely luxury options (private commode tents, proper beds, generator for power) but fewer budget choices.

### When it's best
Any time outside summer. But the experience is best in March, April, October, and November when the weather is right and you're almost guaranteed to have sections of the dunes entirely to yourself.

### The honest drawback
The logistics. Getting there independently is difficult; you're essentially obliged to book a guided trip, which limits flexibility and adds cost. If you want to change plans mid-trip, you can't easily.

## Which erg for which traveller?

**Erg Chebbi (Merzouga) is right if:**
- You have 3–4 days from Marrakech
- This is your first time in the Moroccan desert
- You're travelling solo or as a couple without a fixed operator
- You want a wide range of accommodation choices
- Budget is a real consideration

**Erg Chigaga is right if:**
- You have 5+ days
- You've already done Merzouga and want something different
- Solitude is more important to you than dune height
- You're comfortable with (and can afford) a guided expedition
- You want a more "expedition" feel to the desert experience

---

## Can you skip the comparison and just pick?

If you're planning your first Morocco trip and have 3–4 days: **go to Merzouga**. It's easier, cheaper, and the experience is genuinely excellent. The "too many tourists" concern is real but manageable with good timing (early morning, walking away from camp).

If you've been before or have more time: **Erg Chigaga** offers something Merzouga can't — the feeling of having the desert to yourself.
