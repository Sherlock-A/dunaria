The Moroccan desert is the single most memorable part of most trips to Morocco. The first time you watch the dunes of Erg Chebbi shift from golden to amber to deep red at sunset — that image stays with you. This guide brings together everything you need to plan your own adventure, without the guesswork.

## Which desert to choose?

Morocco has two main desert destinations, and the choice matters.

**Merzouga / Erg Chebbi** is the classic choice: a massive sea of orange dunes rising up to 150 m, accessible by road from Marrakech in about 8 hours. Most organised tours go here. The infrastructure is solid — good camps at every budget, quad rental, camel treks — but it can feel busy in high season.

**M'Hamid / Erg Chigaga** is the wilder alternative. The dunes aren't quite as dramatic as Erg Chebbi, but the solitude is real. Getting there requires a 4x4, and most visitors go with an organised expedition. If you have 5+ days and want to see fewer people, this is your desert.

**Quick answer**: If you have 3–4 days from Marrakech, go to Merzouga. If you have more time and want something more remote, consider Erg Chigaga.

## Best time to visit

Spring (March–May) and autumn (October–November) are the sweet spots: warm days, cool nights, and no extreme temperatures. July and August can hit 48 °C — viable but brutal. Winter days are pleasant (15–22 °C), though desert nights dip below freezing in January.

For full details, see the [month-by-month guide to Morocco desert weather](/en/blog/best-time-morocco-desert).

## Classic itineraries from Marrakech

### 3 days: Marrakech → desert → Marrakech
The most popular option. Day 1: drive south through Aït Ben Haddou and Ouarzazate. Day 2: the Dadès and Todra gorges, arriving at Merzouga by evening. Day 3: camel ride at sunrise, then the long drive back. It's fast but doable — see the [complete 3-day itinerary](/en/blog/marrakech-desert-3-day-itinerary) for stops and logistics.

### 4 days: South Morocco circuit
An extra day lets you breathe. You can spend more time in the gorges, add a dawn ride in the dunes, and drive back via a different route. The [4-day southern Morocco route](/en/blog/4-day-southern-morocco-itinerary) covers this in detail.

## Getting to Merzouga

Three options from Marrakech:

1. **Organised tour** — most hassle-free, driver included, costs €150–300 for 3 days depending on quality. The standard for first-timers.
2. **Rental car** — most flexible, ~€50–80/day for a normal car (4x4 not required on the main road). Drive time is 7–9 hours depending on stops.
3. **CTM bus** — cheap (€15–20 one way), but takes 10–11 hours with a change in Errachidia. Not comfortable for luggage-heavy travellers.

Full breakdown in [how to get to Merzouga from Marrakech](/en/blog/how-to-get-to-merzouga).

## Where to sleep in the desert

The range goes from basic Berber camp (shared toilets, simple meals, €15–25/person) to luxury glamping with private en-suite bathrooms, a pool, and a four-course dinner under a sky with zero light pollution. The experience in a good mid-range camp (€40–70/person) is often the highlight of the entire Morocco trip.

What actually matters: location relative to the dunes, quality of the dinner, and whether the camp is genuinely in the sand or just on the edge of the village. Read the [complete sleep-in-the-desert guide](/en/blog/sleep-in-morocco-desert-camps) before you book.

## What to pack

The desert demands more specific packing than most destinations. Mornings and evenings require a proper jacket even in spring. Sand gets into everything electronic. And those thin-soled sneakers will be uncomfortable on the dunes.

## Is it safe?

Straightforwardly yes. Morocco's southern desert route — Marrakech to Merzouga via Ouarzazate — is one of the most-travelled tourist routes in the country, with no significant security incidents in recent years. The main practical risks are traffic (poor road conditions in some sections) and heat (very real in summer). See [is Morocco desert safe?](/en/blog/is-morocco-desert-safe) for the full picture.

## Activities in the desert

Beyond the camel ride (which takes about 1 hour and is genuinely worthwhile), there's quad biking in the dunes, sandboarding, 4x4 excursions into the deeper desert, and — if you time it right — Gnawa music around the fire at a good camp. The [activities guide](/en/blog/things-to-do-morocco-desert) covers what's worth doing and what to skip.

## How much does it cost?

A realistic 3-day Marrakech–desert budget ranges from €150 (budget: bus, basic camp, meals at local restaurants) to €500+ (private driver, luxury camp, all meals included). The biggest swing is the camp: budget camps charge €15–25/person, luxury camps €100–200/person.
