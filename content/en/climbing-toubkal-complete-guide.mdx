Jebel Toubkal, at 4,167 meters, is the roof of North Africa and the second highest peak on the entire African continent. Its standard summer ascent requires no mountaineering technique and is accessible to anyone with good physical fitness. This makes it one of the most attainable "4000ers" in the world.

---

## Essential facts

- **Summit altitude:** 4,167 m
- **Starting point:** Imlil (1,740 m) → elevation gain: ~2,400 m
- **Duration:** 2 days (with night at the refuge)
- **Total distance:** ~18 km round trip
- **Difficulty:** Medium-high (physical fitness required, not technical)
- **Recommended season:** July to October (snow-free)

---

## The standard route

### Day 1: Imlil → Toubkal Refuge (3,207 m)

**Distance:** ~8 km | **Elevation gain:** +1,467 m | **Time:** 5-6 hours

The route climbs through the Ait Mizane valley passing the village of Aroumd. Above the Neltner (the local name for the refuge), the landscape changes from green to rock and snow. The refuge is well-equipped: dormitories, dining room, direct views of Toubkal's north face.

**Tip:** Don't attempt Day 1 and the summit on the same day. Acclimatization is necessary even at these moderate altitudes.

### Day 2: Refuge → Summit → Imlil

**Departure:** 5:00-6:00 AM to avoid afternoon heat and clouds
**Summit:** 7:30-9:00 AM at a steady pace
**Return to Imlil:** 2:00-4:00 PM

The climb from the refuge to the summit takes 2.5 to 4 hours depending on fitness. The final section has a lot of loose scree. The summit has views of the Anti-Atlas, the Sahara on clear days, and toward the Atlantic.

## Required gear

**Essential (any level):**
- Mountain boots with good grip (not trail running shoes)
- Trekking poles
- Warm layers (summit can be -5°C even in August)
- Windbreaker or rain jacket
- Water for 2 liters per person per stage
- High-UV sunglasses

**In winter (November-April) add:**
- Crampons and ice axe (mandatory)
- Prior experience in snow conditions

---

## Guide: yes or no?

The route is well-marked in summer. You can ascend without a guide if you have mountain experience and the weather is clear.

**Hiring a guide is worthwhile if:**
- It's your first high-altitude experience
- You're traveling solo
- You want multi-day routes through the Atlas valleys
- You're going in winter or snowy conditions

Certified guides from Imlil charge 400-600 MAD/day. You can hire them directly in the village or through the guesthouses.

---

## The Toubkal Refuge

The Toubkal Refuge (also called Refuge Neltner) is managed by the CAF (Club Alpin Français). It accommodates about 60 people in bunk dormitories. Dinner and breakfast are available (basic but hot cooking). 

**Booking:** Mandatory in July and August. Can be booked by email directly with the refuge or through Imlil agencies.

**Approximate price:** 200-250 MAD per night in shared dormitory.
