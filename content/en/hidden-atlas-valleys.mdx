Ourika and Toubkal get most of the Atlas visitors. But the Moroccan Atlas is a 700 km chain with dozens of valleys that few travelers ever discover. This guide presents the most interesting ones for anyone who wants to leave the standard circuits behind.

---

## The Azzaden Valley

Parallel to the Ait Mizane valley (the Toubkal one), the Azzaden is longer, deeper and almost completely free of organized tourism. You can reach it by trekking from Imlil in 2-3 days or by descending from the ridge separating both valleys.

**Why go:** Villages with no souvenir shops or tourist restaurants. Accommodation in family homes (unregistered gîtes d'étape, symbolic prices). Views of Toubkal from the northern side.

**How to get there:** From Imlil with a guide (essential; no signage and terrain can be confusing).

---

## Aït Bougmez Valley ("The Happy Valley")

Named by its inhabitants, Aït Bougmez is in the Central High Atlas, 6-7 hours from Marrakech via the N8. It's probably the greenest and best-preserved valley in the entire Atlas.

**What makes it special:**
- Communal granaries (agadirs) built on rock ledges
- Terraced fields of wheat, corn and wildflowers
- M'Goun peak (4,071 m) as the backdrop — second highest in the Atlas
- Almost no passing tourists (the access road was only recently paved)

**How to get there:** Route from Marrakech via Azilal (CTM bus or grand taxi to Azilal + local taxi to Tabant, the main valley village). Or included in multi-day traverses from Imlil.

---

## The Ounila Valley

Between Ouarzazate and the Tichka, the Ounila river creates a lateral valley off the Tichka that few travelers know. The ksar of Aït Ben Haddou is fed by this valley. Villages further up are virtually intact and unrestored.

**Ideal for:** Photographers, earth architecture lovers, travelers with a car who want to detour an hour from the N9 for something genuine.

---

## How to access the hidden valleys

Most of these valleys have no public transport access. Options are:
1. **Rental car + offline GPS** (Maps.me has good coverage of Atlas secondary roads)
2. **Private tour with a mountain guide** who knows the area
3. **Multi-day walking traverse** connecting the valleys (slowest and most immersive option)

For those who prefer the established Atlas, see the [Imlil guide](/en/blog/imlil-atlas-base-guide) and the [Toubkal guide](/en/blog/climbing-toubkal-complete-guide).
