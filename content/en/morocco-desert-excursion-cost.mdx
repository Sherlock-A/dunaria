How much does a Morocco desert trip cost? The honest answer: anywhere from €150 to €800+ for 3 days, depending almost entirely on how you travel. Here are real prices for 2026, broken down by category.

## Transport from Marrakech

**Budget: CTM bus Marrakech → Erfoud + grand taxi to Merzouga**
- Bus: €15–20 one way
- Grand taxi (shared): €8–12/person one way
- **Total transport (round trip)**: €45–65/person

**Mid-range: Shared group tour (3 days, driver included)**
- Typical price: €120–180/person (group of 4–6)
- Includes: driver, fuel, guided stops at Aït Ben Haddou and gorges
- Not included: accommodation (usually separate)

**Comfort: Private tour (3 days)**
- 2 people: €280–400/person
- 4 people: €200–280/person
- Includes: private vehicle (usually Toyota Land Cruiser), driver-guide, often accommodation

**DIY: Rental car**
- Car rental: €45–80/day × 3 days = €135–240
- Fuel (Marrakech–Merzouga–Marrakech, ~1,100 km): approximately €80–100
- Toll roads: €8–12 total
- **Total per car**: ~€225–350; split between 2–4 people

## Accommodation

### Night 1–2 (en route: Ouarzazate area or gorges)

| Option | Price/room |
|---|---|
| Basic guesthouse | €20–35 |
| Mid-range riad | €50–90 |
| Luxury riad | €100–200 |

### Night 3: Desert camp

| Camp category | Price/person | What's included |
|---|---|---|
| Basic Berber camp | €15–25 | Shared tent, dinner, breakfast, camel ride |
| Mid-range camp | €40–75 | Private tent, dinner, breakfast, camel ride |
| Luxury glamping | €90–200 | Private tent + bathroom, 3-course dinner, service |

The biggest variable in the whole trip is this number. A good mid-range camp costs €50–60/person and delivers an excellent experience. Spending €120 buys a private en-suite bathroom and better food — genuinely worthwhile for a special occasion.

## Food and meals

Most accommodation includes breakfast. Dinner at desert camps is almost always included in the room rate. For driving days:

- Lunch at a roadside restaurant: €6–12/person (tagine + drink)
- Street food in towns: €2–5
- Supermarket snacks: budget €5–10/day for snacks and drinks in the car

## Total budget by travel style (3 days, per person)

### Budget traveller: €150–200
- Bus transport (€50 round trip)
- Basic guesthouse on route (€25/night × 2)
- Basic desert camp (€20/night)
- Meals mostly at local restaurants (€40 total)
- No activities beyond included camel ride

### Mid-range traveller: €350–500
- Shared group tour including transport (€150)
- Mid-range riad on route (€60/room shared × 2)
- Mid-range desert camp (€60/person)
- Lunch at decent restaurants (€60 total)
- 1 quad biking session (€30)

### Comfort/luxury traveller: €600–900
- Private tour with vehicle (€300–400)
- Good riad en route (€80–100/room × 2)
- Luxury desert camp (€120–150/person)
- All meals included or at good restaurants
- Private guided activities

## What's actually worth spending more on?

**Yes**: The desert camp. Moving from basic (€20) to mid-range (€60) makes a tangible difference in sleep quality, food, and overall comfort. The stars and the dunes are identical.

**Yes**: The drive. Paying for a private tour vs bus means you stop at Aït Ben Haddou properly instead of rushing past it.

**Maybe**: The luxury camp. If it's a honeymoon or special occasion, the €120+ option delivers. For a standard trip, mid-range is the sweet spot.

**Skip**: Expensive camel rides sold by village touts (€30–50) — most camps include a 45-minute ride for free.

---

## Things that cost less than you expect

- Entry to Aït Ben Haddou: €1
- Walking through the Todra gorge: free
- Merzouga village: free to explore
- Stargazing from your camp: free (and extraordinary)
