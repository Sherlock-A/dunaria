Four days makes the Marrakech–Merzouga circuit a pleasure rather than a race. You gain time at the gorges, a second sunrise in the dunes, and arrive back in Marrakech without feeling destroyed. This is the version most experienced travellers of southern Morocco recommend.

## Overview

- **Total distance**: ~1,100 km round trip
- **Driving**: 14–16 hours spread over 4 days (3–5 hours/day)
- **Best for**: Anyone who has the time; couples, small groups, families with older children
- **Budget estimate**: €300–400/person (mid-range everything); €600–800/person (private car, quality accommodation)

---

## Day 1: Marrakech → Aït Ben Haddou → Ouarzazate

**Drive**: ~250 km, 3.5 hours

Leave Marrakech at a reasonable hour (8:00–9:00am). The N9 climbs over the **High Atlas via Tizi n'Tichka** — Morocco's most scenic mountain road. Stop at any of the viewpoints: the pass itself at 2,260 m, Telouet (for those who want to see the abandoned Glaoui kasbah, an extra 20 km detour), or just pull off at a natural viewpoint.

### Aït Ben Haddou
Arrive mid-morning for 1.5–2 hours in the UNESCO-listed ksar. The site is smaller than it looks in photos — you can see everything comfortably in 90 minutes. Cross the river (stepping stones or small plank bridge), walk through the lower part of the village, and climb to the upper towers for the view.

Lunch in the small restaurants outside the ksar walls. Tagine runs €5–10/person.

### Ouarzazate (afternoon and overnight)
Arrive in Ouarzazate by early afternoon. If you're a film buff, the **Atlas Corporation Studios** are worth an hour (€12 entry) — the sets include Jerusalem streets, Egyptian temples, and ancient desert cityscapes. The **Taourirt Kasbah** in the centre is free to walk around.

**Sleep**: Ouarzazate is the most comfortable sleeping option on the route outside Marrakech. Good riads: €50–100/room.

## Day 2: Ouarzazate → Dadès Gorge → Todra Gorge → Tinghir

**Drive**: ~200 km, 3 hours (without gorge time)

### Dadès Gorge
Leave Ouarzazate by 8:30–9:00am. Drive east along the **Route des Kasbahs** (N10) — a road lined with crumbling earth kasbahs and palmeries. The Dadès gorge entrance is near Boumalne Dadès (about 2 hours from Ouarzazate).

Drive 25–30 km into the gorge: the road climbs alongside a river, with the canyon walls closing in on both sides. Stop at the **Monkey Fingers** rock formation — a group of bizarre eroded pillars. Walk among them, explore a bit, then continue to a viewpoint further up if you have time.

*Time here*: 1–1.5 hours.

### Todra Gorge
Backtrack from the Dadès to the N10 and continue east to Tinghir (1 hour), then drive 15 km north into the **Todra Gorge**. The walls narrow to just 10 metres apart and rise 300 m on both sides. A clear river runs through the floor.

Spend 45–60 minutes walking through the gorge. The early afternoon light illuminates the west wall dramatically. There are cafes at the entrance; avoid eating at the "tourist" terraces and walk past them to local spots.

**Sleep**: Tinghir has decent small hotels (€30–50/room). Some people sleep in the gorge itself at a basic auberge — a more unusual experience.

## Day 3: Tinghir → Merzouga → Desert camp

**Drive**: ~200 km, 2.5–3 hours

An easy driving day, giving you a relaxed arrival at Merzouga with time to explore.

### Rissani and Erfoud
The two market towns before the desert. Rissani has an important weekly Berber market (Tue/Thu/Sun) where locals trade livestock, produce, and crafts. Erfoud has the last reliable petrol station — fill up here.

### Merzouga arrival (midday)
You arrive at the edge of the dunes with the whole afternoon ahead. Options:

- **Quad biking**: 1–2 hours in the dunes, guided from the village (€20–40/quad)
- **Afternoon camel ride**: longer than the typical sunset trek, into less-visited areas
- **Simply walk into the dunes**: Erg Chebbi is big — 20 minutes of walking from the village edge puts you in the real sand

**Sunset camel trek** (late afternoon): The standard 60-minute trek to a dune top. Worth doing even if you're not a camel person.

**Sleep**: Desert camp. With 4 days, you can afford a mid-range or luxury option — the difference in comfort and food quality is significant. Book 2–3 weeks ahead in spring and autumn.

## Day 4: Sunrise → Merzouga → Marrakech

**Drive**: ~550 km, 7.5–9 hours

### Sunrise (5:00–6:30am)
The second advantage of a 4-day trip: you already know the dunes from the previous afternoon. Walk to a different dune for the sunrise — preferably further from the camp to get away from other groups. The light at 6:15am in April is extraordinary.

Breakfast at camp. Leave by 8:30–9:00am to have a comfortable drive back.

### Return route
The most straightforward: retrace the N10 to Ouarzazate, then take the N9 over the High Atlas back to Marrakech (8–9 hours total, minus stops).

**Alternative northern route** (adds 45 min but is more interesting): Erfoud → Midelt → Azrou → Ifrane → Fes direction, then south back to Marrakech via the Ziz valley. Recommended if you've done the southern route before or want to see the cedar forests of the Middle Atlas.

**Arrive in Marrakech**: 5–7pm, depending on stops and start time.

## 4-day vs 3-day: what the extra day actually gives you

| | 3 days | 4 days |
|---|---|---|
| Stress level | High — always rushing | Manageable |
| Time at Aït Ben Haddou | 1 hour | 2 hours |
| Gorge time | Quick stops | Real exploration |
| Desert arrival | Late afternoon, rushed | Midday, relaxed |
| Sunrises in the dunes | 1 | 2 |
| Return driving day | Brutal (550 km) | Manageable (550 km) |

If you have the flexibility, add the day. The road between Marrakech and the Sahara is not just a means to an end — it's one of the best drives in North Africa.
