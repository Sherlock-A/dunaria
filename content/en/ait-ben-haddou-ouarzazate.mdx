Most travellers to the Moroccan desert pass through Aït Ben Haddou and Ouarzazate on Day 1 of the southern circuit. Both are genuinely worth the stop — not just as photo opportunities, but as extraordinary examples of southern Moroccan earthen architecture. Here's what to do, how long to budget, and what's often overlooked.

## Aït Ben Haddou

### What it is
A fortified village (ksar) built entirely of pisé — compacted earth mixed with straw and stone. The UNESCO listing is well-earned: the ksar represents the traditional pre-Saharan architecture of the Draa Valley, with the tallest kasbahs in the region. The whole ensemble is remarkably intact for a structure that has been inhabited for over a thousand years.

If it looks familiar, it should: Aït Ben Haddou has been a location for dozens of films and TV productions. *Gladiator* (the slave market), *Lawrence of Arabia*, *The Last Temptation of Christ*, *Game of Thrones* (Astapor and Yunkai), *Babel*, *Jewel of the Nile*, *Prince of Persia* — all partially filmed here.

### Visiting
The ksar sits above the **Mellah river**, which you cross via stepping stones or a basic wooden bridge. The site is split between the inhabited lower village (a handful of families still live here) and the upper fortifications.

**Lower village**: A maze of narrow alleyways lined with earthen walls, artisan shops, and guesthouses. Some shops are persistent but not aggressive. Take your time walking through — the light and textures reward slow exploration.

**Upper kasbahs**: A steep climb (10–15 minutes) but worth it for the panoramic view over the river valley and the village rooftops. The kasbah towers are partially restored; you can climb into some sections.

**Far bank viewpoint**: Cross back over the river and walk 200 m downstream for the classic photograph — the entire ksar reflected in the river with the palmerie behind it, or just the whole village against the sky. Early morning light (before 10am) is best.

### Practical details
- **Entry**: 10 MAD (approximately €1)
- **Time needed**: 1.5–2 hours for a proper visit; 45 minutes if rushed
- **Best time**: Early morning (9–10am) before tour buses arrive; late afternoon light is also excellent but crowds are back
- **Guides**: Available at the entrance (€10–15 for a 1-hour tour); not necessary but helpful for context

### What people miss
- The view from the **top of the southern tower** — requires some scrambling but offers the best elevated perspective
- The **small museum** near the entrance documenting the ksar's history and cinema connections
- The **local women's cooperative** selling argan oil products at fair prices (versus the tourist shops near the entrance)

## Ouarzazate

### What it is
A mid-size Moroccan city (population ~75,000) that has become the unlikely capital of the African film industry. Its combination of reliable sunshine, diverse landscapes, and affordable infrastructure has attracted major productions for decades. It's also the junction town between Marrakech and the deep south — the "gateway to the desert."

### Visiting
Ouarzazate doesn't require a full day, but a 2-hour stop reveals more than most people expect.

**Taourirt Kasbah**: A large 19th-century kasbah in the city centre, former seat of the Glaoui clan who controlled the region until 1956. Part is inhabited, part is museum. Entry: 30 MAD. The interior shows the layered architecture of a working kasbah: private apartments, servants' quarters, stables, and guard posts.

**Atlas Corporation Studios**: About 5 km outside the city, this is the second-largest film studio in the world by surface area. The guided tour (€12, about 1 hour) walks through preserved sets from major productions — a Jerusalem market street, an Egyptian temple, a Roman arena. Surprisingly engaging even for non-film enthusiasts.

**The dam and reservoir**: A 10-minute drive from the centre, the Mansour Eddahbi dam creates a large reservoir. The view from the dam road toward the mountains is good; occasionally you'll see flamingos on the water.

### Practical details
- **Taourirt Kasbah hours**: 9am–6pm daily; 30 MAD entry
- **Atlas Studios hours**: Daily 8am–6pm; €12 entry; guided tour in French, English, Spanish
- **Restaurants**: Chez Dimitri (historically notable, open since 1928) and several good tagine spots on Boulevard Mohamed V
- **ATMs**: Available in the city centre

## Combining both in one day

The efficient route: leave Marrakech early → Tizi n'Tichka pass → Aït Ben Haddou (arrive ~11:30am, 1.5 hours) → lunch at Aït Ben Haddou or Ouarzazate → Atlas Studios or Taourirt Kasbah (1.5 hours) → continue south.

If you plan to sleep in Ouarzazate, you can do both sites more leisurely and start fresh toward the gorges the next morning — this is the better option.
