The Ourika Valley is the first piece of the Atlas that most travelers from Marrakech see. At just 65 km with no logistical difficulty, the contrast is immediate: from the red, heat-laden medina to a green valley with a river running between orchards and earth houses.

---

## Why Ourika works as a day trip

The valley has everything you need for a full day without exhaustion:
- Green landscape, water, coolness (especially in summer)
- Weekly market at Tnine-Ourika (Mondays)
- Setti Fatma waterfalls at the end of the route
- Restaurants with terraces over the river
- No technical hiking required

It's perfect for families, travelers who don't want intense trekking, or as a first contact with the Atlas before longer adventures.

---

## How to get to the Ourika Valley

**Shared grand taxi:** From Bab Doukkala (Marrakech), take a grand taxi to Ourika or Tnine-Ourika (15-20 MAD per person, about 45 min). Taxis go to Tnine-Ourika market; to reach Setti Fatma you need to change to a local taxi.

**Private taxi or rental car:** 250-400 MAD round trip with waiting time. The road is fully paved.

**Organized tour from Marrakech:** Many agencies offer Ourika excursions for 100-150 MAD per person by minibus. They include breakfast on the way and a herbalist visit (part of the commercial tour).

---

## What to see in the valley

### Tnine-Ourika (km 35)
The Monday market is genuinely local: valley farmers selling vegetables, animals, spices and crafts. Not tourist-oriented. If you're there on a Monday, it's worth stopping for half an hour.

### The Ourika river
The road follows the river for dozens of kilometers. In spring, the water runs fast and silty from the snow; in summer, it's clear and cool. There are restaurants with terraces literally over the water.

### Setti Fatma (km 65, end of the road)
The highest village in the valley that the road reaches. From here a 45-60 minute trail climbs along the riverbank to a series of 7 linked waterfalls. The last section involves scrambling over rocks (no technical difficulty, but closed shoes are better).

The waterfalls are impressive in spring; in August the flow decreases but the landscape is still beautiful.

---

## When to go

- **Spring (March-May):** Best time. The river is flowing, fruit trees are blossoming, colors are vivid.
- **Summer (June-August):** The valley's coolness contrasts with Marrakech's heat. Perfect for swimming in the water.
- **Autumn (September-November):** Good option. Fewer tourists.
- **Winter (December-February):** Upper valley may have snow. Fewer open restaurants.

**Avoid:** Summer weekends (July-August). The valley fills with Marrakech residents and traffic access can be complicated.
