When people say "Morocco desert," they usually picture the towering orange dunes of Erg Chebbi near Merzouga. But there's a second, wilder desert to the west: Erg Chigaga, near M'Hamid, accessed through Zagora. Choosing between them changes not just the dunes you see, but the whole atmosphere of the trip.

## The short answer

**Choose Merzouga** if you have 3–4 days from Marrakech, want tall impressive dunes, and prefer solid tourist infrastructure (good camp options, easy logistics).

**Choose Zagora / Erg Chigaga** if you have 5+ days, want genuine remoteness, and don't mind paying more for a guided expedition.

---

## Erg Chebbi (Merzouga)

### The dunes
Erg Chebbi is one of the largest ergs (sand seas) in Morocco — about 28 km long and up to 150 m high. The sand is a deep, warm orange. On a clear April morning at sunrise, it's as good as advertised.

The famous photo — a line of camels silhouetted against an enormous dune at sunset — comes from here.

### Access
Fully paved road all the way from Marrakech. A normal car is fine. The drive takes 8–9 hours with stops at Aït Ben Haddou and the gorges. No guide required to get there or to ride a camel in the dunes — you just show up.

### Infrastructure
The most developed desert tourism setup in Morocco. Dozens of camps at every price point, from basic Berber tents at €15/person to luxury glamping at €150+/person. Quad rental, sandboarding, 4x4 excursions, camel treks — all available directly from the camp or the nearby village.

### Crowds
High season (April, October) gets busy. You'll share the dunes with other groups, and some of the more photogenic spots are never empty. For a genuine solitude experience, you need to walk at least 30–40 minutes from the main camps — or go very early.

## Erg Chigaga (Zagora / M'Hamid)

### The dunes
Smaller than Erg Chebbi — around 40 km long but lower dunes (50–60 m typically). The sand color tends towards a more golden-brown. Less dramatic in photos but more intimate on the ground.

### Access
Zagora is 350 km from Marrakech by road (about 5–6 hours). From Zagora, M'Hamid is another hour. From M'Hamid, Erg Chigaga is 50 km of piste (unpaved track) — a 4x4 is required and driving it independently without local knowledge is not recommended.

Most people visit Erg Chigaga with a guided expedition: a local operator provides vehicles, guide, and camp setup in the deep dunes.

### Infrastructure
Much thinner than Merzouga. There are fewer camps, less choice for solo travellers, and fewer services. This is both a feature and a drawback — the lack of infrastructure is exactly why it feels remote.

### Crowds
Genuinely quiet, even in peak season. You may have a large section of dunes entirely to yourself.

## Side-by-side comparison

| | Merzouga (Erg Chebbi) | Zagora (Erg Chigaga) |
|---|---|---|
| Dune height | Up to 150 m | Up to 60 m |
| Drive from Marrakech | 8–9 hours | 6–7 hours (+ 1h piste) |
| 4x4 needed? | No | Yes (for the dunes) |
| Camp options | Many, all budgets | Few, mostly guided |
| Crowds in high season | Moderate-high | Low |
| Solo traveller viability | Very easy | Harder |
| Minimum days | 3 | 5 |
| Budget/person (3 days) | €150–400 | €250–500 |

## Who should go where?

**Go to Merzouga if:**
- You have 3–4 days and are coming from Marrakech
- You want the "classic" Sahara photo experience
- You're travelling solo or in a couple without a guide
- Budget is a consideration

**Go to Erg Chigaga if:**
- You have at least 5 days
- Solitude matters more to you than the height of the dunes
- You're comfortable booking a guided expedition
- You've already done Merzouga and want something different

---

## Can you do both?

Technically yes, but it requires at least 7–8 days and significant extra driving. The two ergs are roughly 250 km apart by road. A circular route — Marrakech → Erg Chebbi → Zagora → Erg Chigaga → Marrakech — works well as a week-long trip, but it's a lot of driving. Most people choose one.
