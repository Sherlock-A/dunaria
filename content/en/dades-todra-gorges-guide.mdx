The Dadès and Todra gorges are the southern border of the High Atlas: the point where the mountains meet the prelude to the desert. Both lie on the natural route between Marrakech and Merzouga, making them a must-stop on any tour of southern Morocco.

They are different in character: the Dadès is a landscape in motion (tight bends, kasbahs on the slopes, palm groves); the Todra is a direct blow of scale (vertical walls 300 m high, barely 10 m apart).

---

## Dadès Gorges

### What they are

The Dadès river has carved a canyon over millions of years that climbs from Boumalne Dades toward the High Atlas. The most spectacular section starts about 25 km from Boumalne and culminates in the bends known as "the Thousand Kasbahs" or "the monkey's finger."

The road climbs to about 1,800 m with views of red earth kasbahs against the green valley backdrop and beige desert below.

### How to get there

**From Marrakech:** 4h30 by car via the N9 through the Tichka pass and Ouarzazate. There are CTM buses from Ouarzazate to Boumalne.

**From Merzouga:** 3h by road. The Dadès is midway along the route.

### Where to stay

Several gîtes and small hotels in the gorges. The most valued are those inside the canyon with views of the rock walls. Prices are low (150-350 MAD/night) and the nights are extremely quiet.

## Todra Gorges

### What they are

The Todra river rises in the High Atlas and creates a canyon with near-vertical walls that at its narrowest point is barely 10 meters wide and 300 meters high. It's one of the most geometrically impressive spaces in North Africa.

The most spectacular section covers barely 2 km on foot. Then the canyon opens up and the route continues toward the interior of the Atlas.

### Rock climbing

The Todra is an internationally recognized rock climbing destination. There are over 150 equipped routes on the vertical walls, with grades from 5b to 8c. Tinghir agencies organize climbing with gear and guide.

---

## Dadès + Todra together: how to plan it

The classic route links both gorges in 2 days:

**Day 1:** Marrakech → Tichka → Ouarzazate → Boumalne Dadès → up into the gorges (night in the Dadès canyon)
**Day 2:** Dadès Gorges → Tinghir → Todra Gorges → Erfoud → Merzouga
