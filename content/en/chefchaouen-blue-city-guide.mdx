Chefchaouen is the blue city of northern Morocco, nestled in the Rif mountains at 1,600 meters. Its alleys painted in various shades of indigo and sky blue have generated millions of Instagram photos, but the city has more depth than its social media suggests.

---

## Why it's painted blue

The origin of Chefchaouen's blue has several non-exclusive explanations:

- **Jewish tradition**: the Jewish community that arrived fleeing Spain in 1492 painted their houses blue as a symbol of heaven and spirituality. When they settled in Chefchaouen, they spread the practice.
- **Mosquito repellent**: a pragmatic theory. The indigo used in the paint has properties that repel insects.
- **Urban identity**: when the city became a tourist destination, the blue was reinforced as a hallmark. Today it's a mix of historical heritage and urban marketing.

The reality: the city was blue before tourism, but tourism has intensified and standardized the blue. The most photogenic tones are partly a recent addition.

## What to see in Chefchaouen

### The medina and kasbah
The medina is small (walkable in 2-3 hours) but visually very dense. The 15th-century kasbah has a quiet interior garden and a small tower with views over the blue rooftops and Rif mountains.

### Plaza Uta el-Hammam
The center of the city's social life. Café, restaurants, mint tea and the Grand Mosque with its octagonal minaret (unique in Morocco).

### Ras el-Ma (spring and river)
10 minutes walk from the medina, women from the upper neighborhoods still wash clothes in the river. A more authentic coda to the tourist visit.

### Trekking in the Rif
Chefchaouen's surroundings have trekking routes to Rif peaks with views of the medina. The climb to the Spanish mosque (an abandoned mosque on a hill) gives the most photogenic panorama of the city.

## Is the detour worth it?

Yes, with caveats. Chefchaouen is a beautiful city and quieter than Marrakech. But expectations need adjustment:

- **Photo saturation**: the bluest streets are the most crowded. Arriving very early or late afternoon is the difference between peace and crowds.
- **Actual size**: the medina is small. Half a day covers the main sights. Those who stay 2-3 days come for the trekking and mountain atmosphere.
- **Not an official imperial city**: not on the classic circuit but perfectly complements 7-10 days in the north.

---

## How to get there

- **From Fes**: 4h on CTM bus. Several departures daily.
- **From Tangier**: 3h by bus. The most common option for those arriving by ferry from Spain.
- **From Tetouan**: 1h by grand taxi. Tetouan is another northern city worth at least half a day.

---

## Where to stay

Chefchaouen has a good range of riads and guesthouses inside the medina. Prices lower than Marrakech (150-350 MAD/night for a double room with breakfast). Fills up quickly in peak season (July-August, Easter).
