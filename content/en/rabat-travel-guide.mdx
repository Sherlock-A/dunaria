Rabat is Morocco's most underestimated capital. While Casablanca attracts business and Marrakech mass tourism, Rabat has something of the best of both: modern infrastructure, a well-preserved historic medina, artistic presence and a pace of life that doesn't exhaust you.

---

## Why visit Rabat

For travelers arriving from Fes or Marrakech, Rabat is a respite. The medina has vendors but not the insistent pressure of other imperial cities. Monuments are accessible. And the capital atmosphere — with embassies, universities and student cafés — gives a different profile from the rest of the country.

Rabat is also the only point on the imperial circuit where Roman, Islamic and contemporary history literally coexist within a few kilometers.

---

## What to see in Rabat

### Oudayas Kasbah
The city's most photogenic spot. The kasbah is a 12th-century fortress on the headland where the Bouregreg river meets the Atlantic. Inside: white and blue alleys (similar to Chefchaouen but without the crowds), a 17th-century Andalusian garden and a café with views of the Atlantic Ocean.

**Best time:** Sunset, with Atlantic side-lighting.

### Hassan Tower and Mohammed V Mausoleum
Hassan Tower is the unfinished minaret of a 12th-century mosque. It stands only 44 meters of the planned 86 — Sultan Yacoub Al-Mansur died before completing it. Next to it, the Mohammed V Mausoleum (20th century) holds the remains of the king who obtained Morocco's independence from France (1956). The medieval-modern combination in the same space is unique.

### Chellah
Morocco's strangest medieval necropolis: a walled city containing Roman ruins (the city of Sala Colonia, 1st century BC) with a 14th-century Merinid necropolis built on top. Storks nest in the ruins and the whole complex has been naturalized. A place outside of time.

### Medina
Smaller and less intense than Fes or Marrakech's. Good craft souks (carpets, blue Fes ceramics, babouches). The Mohammed V avenue is the main axis of the new town with well-preserved French colonial Art Deco architecture.

## How to get to Rabat

- **From Casablanca:** ONCF train every 30 min, 1h, ~35 MAD. The most convenient option.
- **From Fes:** Direct train, 3h. Frequent CTM buses also available.
- **From Tangier:** Train or bus, 3-4h.

Rabat has two train stations: Rabat-Ville (city center) and Rabat-Agdal (modern area). Use Rabat-Ville for the medina.

---

## How much time do you need

- **Half day**: arriving from Casablanca or Fes, you can see the Oudayas Kasbah, Hassan Tower and Mausoleum in 4-5 hours.
- **Full day**: add Chellah, the medina and the new town.
- **Overnight**: Rabat has a restaurant and café terrace scene less touristy than Marrakech, with more reasonable prices.
