Essaouira's beaches are long, wide, fine-sand and with an intensely turquoise Atlantic. They're also windy — sometimes very windy. Knowing when and where to go makes the difference between a fantastic beach experience and an uncomfortable afternoon eating sand.

## The main beach of Essaouira

The **Essaouira beach** starts just south of the medina and extends about 8 km to Cap Sim. It's the most accessible: you can walk from the ramparts in 5 minutes.

### Characteristics

- Fine ocre-gold sand
- Shallow entry (good for wading in gradually)
- Wind almost always present (the Atlantic trade wind)
- Lifeguards in high season (July-August)
- Showers and changing rooms on the north section of the beach
- Cafés and restaurants along the beachfront promenade

### When to go

Wind is the key factor. The **trade wind** blows from the northwest with force between June and August — in those months sand flies and sunbathing becomes a challenge. Early morning (before 11am) the wind is more moderate. From 2pm in summer it can be very strong.

In **spring and autumn** (April-May, September-October) the main beach is perfect: moderate wind, cool but swimmable water, pleasant sun. The best season.

In **winter** the beach has a desolate and beautiful atmosphere. Waves can be large. Not bathing season but great for walking.

### Activities

- Horse riding: rentals on the south beach (~100-150 MAD/hour)
- Quad biking: in the south beach zone, away from bathers
- Windsurfing and kitesurfing: north beach area, near the Essaouira headland
- Surfing: suitable for beginners on lower wind days

## Sidi Kaouki: the kitesurfing beach

**Sidi Kaouki** is 27 km south of Essaouira on the P2101 (coast road). It's a smaller beach with a small fishing village and several kitesurfing and windsurfing schools.

### Why Sidi Kaouki

The wind here is more consistent and the beach has less current than Essaouira's main beach. It's the preferred spot for intermediate and advanced kitesurfers. In July and August, when the trade wind blows hard, dozens of kites gather in the water.

It's also a good beach for **swimming more calmly** than the main beach, especially in transition months when Essaouira itself can still be windy.

### How to get there

- **Car or motorbike**: the most comfortable option. 30 minutes from Essaouira on tarmac road.
- **Shared taxi**: from the taxi station at Essaouira's entrance, towards Sidi Kaouki. ~30-40 MAD per person.
- **Bike**: possible but the road has some sections without a shoulder. Better on a calm morning.

## Diabat: the historic beach

**Diabat** is about 4 km south of Essaouira, where the Ksob river meets the Atlantic. It's known among travellers for two things: the ruins of an 18th-century palace and its connection with Jimi Hendrix.

### The palace ruins

Next to the river mouth are the ruins of **Dar Soltane palace**, where Sultan Sidi Mohammed ben Abdallah (the same one who built the medina) had a summer residence. The ruins are partial but the setting is spectacular: sand, palm trees, river and ocean.

### The Jimi Hendrix legend

Hendrix visited Essaouira and Diabat in 1969. According to local legend (widely romanticised), he slept in the palace ruins and the experience inspired him to write *Castles Made of Sand*. The real relationship is probably less epic, but Diabat remains a pilgrimage point for fans.

### How to get there

On foot from Essaouira in 50-60 minutes along the beach (low tide) or along the road (taxi). There's not much in the village of Diabat — it's more of a destination for the walk and ruins.

## Summary: which beach for what?

| If you want... | Best option |
|---|---|
| Convenience and easy access | Essaouira main beach |
| Kitesurfing/windsurfing | Sidi Kaouki |
| Peace and nature | Diabat or Cap Sim |
| Swimming without strong wind | Sidi Kaouki (spring/autumn) |
| History and ruins | Diabat |
