Meknès is Morocco's least visited imperial city, and also the one with the best value-for-experience ratio. An hour from Fes by bus, it concentrates 17th-century monumental architecture, a lively medina without tourist saturation, and the most convenient access to Volubilis, the best-preserved Roman ruins in North Africa.

---

## History: the builder sultan

Meknès was capital of Morocco for barely 40 years (1672-1727), under the reign of Moulay Ismail, the most ambitious sultan in Moroccan history. His project was to build a capital rivaling the Versailles of Louis XIV (his contemporary and reference point). He used slave labor, materials from across Morocco and a grand-scale urban vision.

He died without finishing it, and his successor moved the capital back to Fes. What remained are enormous ruins, monumental gates, granaries and stables that give a sense of what might have been.

---

## What to see in Meknès

### Bab Mansour
Morocco's most imposing city gate, built 1732. 16 meters high, flanked by Corinthian columns taken from Volubilis, with zellij ornamentation mixing green and ochre ceramic. The most reproduced photograph of Meknès.

### Moulay Ismail Mausoleum
The only mausoleum in Morocco open to non-Muslims. The sultanate becomes visible here: fountain courtyards, burial chambers with cedar woodwork and the sultan's tomb. Cool and silent even in summer.

### Medina and Bou Inania Medersa
The Meknès medina has authentic souk life without the pressure of Fes or Marrakech. Vendors are more relaxed, starting prices more reasonable. The Bou Inania Medersa (14th century) has the same decorative quality as Fes's with a tenth of the visitors.

### Royal Granaries and Stables (Heri es-Souani)
Meknès's most surreal ruins: stables for 12,000 horses and granaries capable of feeding the city for 20 years. The vaulted ceilings collapsed in the 1755 earthquake (the same that destroyed Lisbon). The ruined space has a cinematic scale.

## Volubilis: the Roman ruins (30 km)

Volubilis is the largest Roman city in North Africa and one of the best-preserved archaeological sites on the continent. It was inhabited from the 3rd century BC until the 11th century AD, serving as capital of the Roman province of Mauritania Tingitana.

**What to see:**
- Caracalla's Arch (217 AD)
- In-situ Roman mosaics (unique preservation level)
- House of Orpheus with the Orpheus charming animals mosaic
- The forum and basilica

**How to get there:** Grand taxi from Meknès (30 min, ~50 MAD one way). Allow 2-3 hours for the visit.

---

## How to organize the visit

**From Fes:** Leave in the morning, visit Meknès (Bab Mansour, mausoleum, medina), lunch in the medina, go to Volubilis in the afternoon, back to Fes before dark. All in one long day (12h).

**From Marrakech:** Meknès is a natural stop on the Marrakech→Fes journey.

**Meknès alone + overnight:** Medina riads are cheap (150-300 MAD) and the city at night is quieter than Fes.
