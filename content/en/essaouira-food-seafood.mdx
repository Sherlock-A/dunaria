Essaouira has some of the best seafood cooking in Morocco. With the Atlantic metres away and an active fishing fleet, the fish on your plate has only hours between the sea and the table. This guide tells you where to eat well in Essaouira, at what prices and what you can't miss.

## Essaouira's food scene

Local Essaouira cooking blends the **Moroccan inland tradition** (tagines, couscous, pastilla) with the **Atlantic coastal cuisine** (seafood, grilled fish, fish soups). Add the influence of the historic Jewish community (some Sephardic local dishes survive) and hints of Amazigh spices from the region.

What you'll find in restaurants isn't fusion design cooking — it's traditional food with very fresh ingredients.

## The port: the most authentic food experience

The **fishing port** of Essaouira is the most iconic food spot. Two types of stalls:

### The auction stalls

Fishermen sell the day's catch directly. Middlemen buy for restaurants. You can also buy — the protocol is to point at what you want, have it weighed, and carry it to the cooking stall next door.

**What to buy**: sardines (~20 MAD/kg), prawns (~80-120 MAD/kg), gambas (~150-200 MAD/kg), squid (~50-70 MAD/kg), tuna (~60-80 MAD/kg), sea bass and sea bream by season.

**Hours**: the criée starts around 7am-8am and finishes by noon when everything is sold.

### The cooking stalls

Right next to the fish sales stalls, several stalls cook what has just come off the sea. The formula: choose the fish (or buy your own), pay a small cooking fee (~20-30 MAD), and it's served grilled or fried.

With a spend of 80-120 MAD (7-11€) you can eat very well. It's the freshest seafood you'll find in Morocco at that price.

## Medina restaurants

### Côté Plage

On the promenade bordering the north beach, with a terrace facing the Atlantic. Decent service, varied menu (seafood, tagines, salads), mid-range prices (150-250 MAD per person). Among the most touristy but the location justifies it.

**Ideal for**: lunch with views, visitors wanting comfort.

### Les Alizés

In the medina, near Plaza Moulay Hassan. Quality traditional Moroccan cooking: seafood pastilla, sea bass tagine with chermoula, Friday couscous. Prices above average (250-400 MAD/person). Book ahead in high season.

**Ideal for**: special dinner, trying quality Moroccan cooking.

### Restaurant du Port

Right at the port entrance. Specialises in grilled and plancha seafood and fish. Service can be slow but the product is excellent. Mid-range price 150-250 MAD/person.

**Ideal for**: seafood-focused lunches.

## Cafés and breakfasts

### Taros Café

On the corner of Plaza Moulay Hassan, with a first-floor terrace. The most known café in Essaouira among travellers. Bohemian atmosphere, good music, varied teas. Breakfasts (coffee + Moroccan pastries) cost ~50-80 MAD.

### Café de la Place

On the same square, more local. Mint tea, coffee, fruit juices and almond pastries. 20-40 MAD. Where craftsmen breakfast before opening their workshops.

## What to eat that is specific to Essaouira

**Chermoula**: marinade of coriander, garlic, lemon and spices. The base sauce for local fish — fresher and more citrusy than inland sauces.

**Seafood pastilla**: coastal version of pastilla (warka pastry) with a seafood filling instead of the classic pigeon. One of the most refined preparations in local cooking.

**Sardine tagine**: in the Moroccan interior, tagine is lamb or chicken. Here it's fresh sardines with tomato, olives and chermoula. Very local.

**Argan with amlou**: almond, honey and argan oil paste for dipping bread at breakfast. Argan oil from the region is higher quality than what's sold in inland cities.
