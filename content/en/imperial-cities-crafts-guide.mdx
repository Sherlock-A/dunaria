Morocco has one of the most vibrant craft traditions in the Arab world. In the imperial cities, workshops have operated continuously for centuries, and souk shops sell genuine pieces alongside industrial imitations without apparent distinction. This guide helps you navigate.

---

## Specialties by city

### Fes: leather, blue ceramics and copper

**Leather (maroquinerie):**
The Chouara tannery quarter has been producing leather since the 11th century. Genuine fasi leather (from Fes) has a texture and finish that clearly distinguishes it from imitations. Babouches (slippers), wallets, belts and bags are the most in-demand items.

**Blue ceramics (zellige from Fes):**
Hand-painted ceramics with the characteristic cobalt blue in various tones are exclusive to Fes. Production centers are in the Ain Nokbi area (near the potters' quarter). An authentic large piece is heavy — imitations are lighter and the enamel less uniform.

**Copper and brass:**
The blacksmiths' (braziers') quarter produces trays, teapots and lanterns. The sound of hammers on metal is one of the characteristic sounds of the Fes medina.

---

### Marrakech: babouches, carpets, spices

**Atlas carpets:**
Berber Atlas carpets (wool, hand-woven) are the most valuable items in the Marrakech souk. The Maison Berbère in the carpet souk has a very elaborate sales process — they serve tea while showing pieces. Don't buy unless you're sure; the sales pressure is intense.

**Spices from the Attarine souk:**
The spice souk is the medina's most aromatic. Ras el hanout blends (30-50 ingredients), genuine saffron (much "saffron" sold in Morocco is food coloring), black cumin and rose petals. Genuine saffron costs 15-25 MAD per gram — if it costs much less, it's not saffron.

---

### Meknès: wrought iron and wine

**Wrought iron:**
Meknès has a less well-known tradition of artistic blacksmithing. The forged iron lamps, candleholders and decorative doors in the Meknès souks have good quality at lower prices than in Marrakech.

**Meknès wines:**
The Meknès valley is Morocco's main wine region. Les Celliers de Meknès wines (Gris de Boulaouane, Coteaux de l'Atlas) are exported throughout Europe. Wine shops in the new town sell bottles at cellar prices.

---

### Rabat: Rabati carpets and textiles

**Rabati carpets:**
Rabat carpets differ from Atlas ones: dark red or blue base, more formal geometric motifs, higher knot density. More expensive but also more elaborate.

---

## How to buy without mistakes

**Bargaining:** Normal and expected in the souks. Not compulsory — many imperial city shops have fixed prices. When a seller quotes a price, offering 50-60% as a starting point and negotiating from there. If the price seems right, no need to bargain.

**Quality vs. price:** A low price doesn't always indicate imitation, but genuine craftsmanship is rarely cheaper in tourist souks than in artisan cooperatives. Look for the Artisanat du Maroc seal or buy directly at visible cooperatives (Ensemble Artisanal).
