Imlil is a Berber village at 1,740 meters altitude in the heart of the High Atlas, 67 km from Marrakech. It is the starting point for the ascent of Jebel Toubkal (4,167 m) and for trekking routes through the Ait Mizane and Azzaden valleys. It is also, in itself, a place worth staying a day to understand how the Atlas Berber population lives.

---

## How to get there from Marrakech

**Option 1 — Grand taxi:**
- From Marrakech bus station (Bab Doukkala), take a grand taxi to Asni (40 km, ~40 min, ~15-20 MAD/person).
- From Asni, another grand taxi to Imlil (30 min, ~10-15 MAD/person).

**Option 2 — Private taxi:**
- Direct from Marrakech to Imlil: 250-350 MAD for the complete journey. More comfortable and faster (about 1h15).

**Option 3 — Organized tour:**
- Marrakech agencies include transfers in Toubkal ascent packages.

There is no direct public transport from Marrakech to Imlil.

## What to do in Imlil

### Immediate hiking
Several easy trails start from the village itself through the valley. The route to Aroumd (the highest village in the valley, 1h walk) gives a good sense of the landscape and ochre-earth architecture of the Atlas without requiring significant effort.

### Toubkal ascent
See the complete guide to [climbing Toubkal](/en/blog/climbing-toubkal-complete-guide). Imlil is the starting point for all summit approaches.

### Multi-day routes
From Imlil you can do the Azzaden valley traverse (2-3 days) or connect to other Atlas valleys through routes that cross roadless villages. Local guides know these itineraries better than any map.

## Where to sleep

Imlil has family-run guesthouses (maisons d'hôtes). Prices generally include breakfast:

- **Basic range:** 100-150 MAD/night. Simple room, shared bathroom.
- **Mid-range:** 200-300 MAD/night. En-suite room, valley views.
- **Upper range:** 400-600 MAD/night. Converted kasbah, terrace with views, dinner included.

**Tip:** Book in advance in July-August. Outside peak season you can arrive without a reservation and easily find a room.

---

## What to eat

Imlil food is simple and good: chicken or vegetable tagine, couscous (Fridays and weekends), harira soup in the evenings. Most guesthouses serve food; there are also a couple of restaurants near the central square.

The Berber breakfast (rye bread with argan oil and local honey, eggs, mint tea) is one of the best aspects of staying in the village.

---

## When to go

- **Best for trekking:** April-June and September-October.
- **Toubkal ascent without snow:** July-September.
- **Winter:** The village is snowy and very peaceful. Toubkal requires crampons.
- **Ramadan:** Some restaurants close during the day; guesthouse schedules vary.

---

## Practical information

- **Electricity:** Available in most guesthouses, but outages are common. Bring a power bank.
- **Water:** Tap water is drinkable in Imlil (mountain spring).
- **Mobile coverage:** Intermittent. Maroc Telecom has better signal than other carriers.
- **Equipment rental:** Poles, crampons and backpacks can be rented in the central village square.
- **Certified guides:** The mountain guide federation has an office in Imlil. Prices are regulated: ~400-600 MAD/day.
