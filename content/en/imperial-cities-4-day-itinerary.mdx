4 days is enough to do the Moroccan imperial cities circuit if you're efficient. This itinerary covers the essentials without trying to exhaust any city. For real depth, the 7-day format is what actually allows you to do it properly.

---

## Day 1 — Marrakech

**Arrival and afternoon in the medina**

Most international flights arrive in Marrakech. Spend the first afternoon orienting yourself: check in at a medina riad (ideal) or Gueliz, a gentle walk through the souks without buying anything, dinner at Jemaa el Fna square.

**Evening:** The square at its most lively. Gnawa musicians, storytellers, juice vendors. You don't need to buy anything to enjoy the show.

---

## Day 2 — Marrakech: monuments + travel to Fes

**Morning (7-12h): Marrakech**
- Bahia Palace (opens 9am, arrive before the organized tour group)
- Saadian Tombs
- Majorelle Garden (if there's no long queue — it has a capacity limit)

**12h-14h:** Lunch in the medina, light shopping.

**14h-15h:** Grand taxi or bus to the station.

**15h30:** Train Marrakech → Fes. Duration: 7h15. Arrive at 10-11pm. The train has a café car and is very comfortable.

*Faster but more expensive alternative:* CTM night bus (6h30, departs 8pm) or domestic flight (1h, Air Arabia Maroc).

---

## Day 3 — Meknès (morning) + Fes (afternoon)

**7h-12h: Meknès**
Leave Fes toward Meknès by grand taxi (1h). See Bab Mansour, Moulay Ismail Mausoleum and the medina souk. Lunch in Meknès.

**14h-19h: Fes**
Return to Fes. First medina exploration in the afternoon with sunset light: Bab Bou Jeloud, main axis to Bou Inania Medersa.

**8pm:** Dinner at riad or medina restaurant.

---

## Day 4 — Fes in depth + flight

**7h-13h: Fes medina**
- Official guide for Chouara tanneries (hardest to find alone)
- Al-Attarine Medersa and Kairouyine area
- Mellah and Ibn Danan Synagogue

**2pm:** Lunch and shopping (Fes crafts: leather, copper, blue ceramics are the best souvenirs).

**Afternoon:** Flight from Fes-Saïss Airport or bus to Casablanca for international flight.

---

## What doesn't fit in 4 days

- Rabat (needs at least half a day, hard to integrate)
- Volubilis (requires a dedicated day from Meknès/Fes)
- Chefchaouen (2h off the main circuit route)

To include all of that, use the [7-day itinerary](/en/blog/northern-morocco-7-day-itinerary).
