The Moroccan Atlas has routes for all levels, from 2-hour walks accessible in city shoes to 7-day traverses requiring high-mountain experience. If it's your first time in the area, here's everything you need to choose well and enjoy without surprises.

---

## The most accessible routes for beginners

### Setti Fatma Waterfalls (Ourika Valley)
**Time:** 1h-1h30 one way | **Elevation gain:** +200 m | **Difficulty:** Easy-moderate

The trail climbs along the Ourika riverbank to a series of 7 linked waterfalls. The first section is a normal walk; the last 20 minutes involve scrambling over rocks (no technical difficulty). Poles and hiking boots are not required but appreciated.

**Ideal for:** First Atlas excursion, families with children over 8, travelers with no mountain experience.

### Valley circuit from Imlil (1 day)
**Time:** 4-5 hours | **Elevation gain:** +400 m | **Difficulty:** Moderate

From Imlil to the village of Aroumd (1,840 m) and back through the Ait Mizane valley. Landscape of orchards, earth houses and first views of Toubkal. A local guide isn't essential but greatly enriches the experience.

**Ideal for:** Travelers who want to taste the Atlas without the summit pressure.

### Toubkal Refuge (without summit)
**Time:** 5-6 hours | **Elevation gain:** +1,467 m | **Difficulty:** Moderate-high

Ascending to the refuge (3,207 m) without attempting the summit is an excellent option for ambitious beginners. The views from the refuge are spectacular and spending a night at that altitude is memorable. The descent the next day is much easier.

## What gear to bring

**Essential (any level):**
- Closed shoes with non-slip soles. Trekking shoes or light boots.
- Water: minimum 1.5 liters per person for routes under 3 hours; 2-3 liters for long routes.
- Sunscreen and sunglasses. Mountain sun is much more intense.
- Warm layers even if you leave in heat. Temperature changes fast.

**Recommended for routes over 4 hours:**
- Trekking poles (reduce knee stress on descents)
- Energy snacks
- Offline map downloaded (Maps.me has good Atlas coverage)

---

## Do I need a guide?

**For shorter, well-marked routes (Ourika, Imlil-Aroumd circuit):** Not necessary, but a local guide adds cultural context that greatly enriches the visit.

**For Toubkal or any multi-day route:** Highly recommended for your first high-mountain experience. A certified guide from the mountain guide federation costs 400-600 MAD/day and can be hired directly in Imlil.

---

## Required fitness level

For easy-level routes (Ourika): no special requirements. If you can walk an hour on flat ground, you can do these routes.

For the Imlil circuit or Toubkal refuge: you should be able to climb stairs without getting winded and walk 3-4 hours continuously.
