Essaouira is 176 km from Marrakech — 3 hours by road — and it's the most popular coastal day trip from the red city. This article explains the transport options, what you can see in a day and whether it's worth staying overnight.

## Is it worth it as a day trip?

Yes, but with nuances. In one day from Marrakech you can see the medina, ramparts and port, and eat seafood at the port stalls. That's already a complete experience.

What you miss with a day trip:
- The atmosphere of the city at sunset and at night (when the tourists leave is when Essaouira is most authentic)
- The beaches (for swimming or kitesurfing you need more time)
- The chance to explore the thuya workshops and minor souks at leisure

If you have 2 days, Essaouira as a destination in its own right is more rewarding. If you have one day, the trip is highly recommended.

## Transport options

### CTM or Supratours bus

**The most comfortable and economical option.**

- **Departures from Marrakech**: from the CTM terminal (near Jemaa el-Fna) or the Supratours station (next to the gare routière). Several morning departures — the most practical are 7:30am, 8:30am and 10am.
- **Duration**: 3h30 on most departures.
- **Price**: 80-120 MAD (7-11€) per journey.
- **Return**: last departures from Essaouira to Marrakech around 5:30pm-6:30pm. **Verify exact timetables** on the day as they vary by season.
- **Booking**: recommended in July-August and on public holidays. Buses fill up. Buy at the ticket desk 1-2 days ahead or on the CTM website.

### Shared grand taxi

- **From**: Bab Doukkala grands taxis station (Marrakech).
- **Duration**: similar to bus, 3h-3h30.
- **Price**: similar to bus, but depends on whether the taxi fills up (6 passengers) or you pay for the whole taxi.
- **Schedule**: more flexible — when the taxi fills up, it goes. No fixed timetables.
- **Comfort**: taxis are old Mercedes with 6 passengers. Can be uncomfortable on long journeys.

### Private taxi or rental car

- **Taxi from Marrakech to Essaouira**: 600-900 MAD (55-82€) one way. For groups of 3-4 people it's more comfortable and not much more expensive than the bus.
- **Rental car**: allows stops along the way (see below), but parking in Essaouira isn't straightforward — the medina has no vehicle access.

## The drive: interesting stops

If you go by private car or taxi, several interesting stops along the N1 and side roads:

**Chichaoua** (on the N1, km 80): small town with a crafts market for rugs and ceramics. Quick to visit.

**Argan forest**: between Chichaoua and the Essaouira turnoff, the road passes through the Argan Biosphere Reserve area. If you see a women's co-operative by the road, stop — it's the opportunity to buy argan oil directly from the source.

**Goats in the argan trees**: argan trees are the only trees where goats climb to eat the leaves. The image of goats high in the tree is well known in Morocco photos. On the Essaouira-Marrakech road there are areas where goats climb spontaneously — and also spots where shepherds put them up specifically for tourists in exchange for a tip (ask permission before photographing).

## The perfect one-day itinerary

With the 7:30am bus from Marrakech, you arrive in Essaouira around 11am. Here's what you can do:

**11am – 12:30pm**: Arrival, luggage deposit at station left luggage (if needed), walk to medina (10 minutes). First walk along the main streets.

**12:30pm – 1:30pm**: Lunch at port stalls. Choose the fish, eat it grilled. The most memorable and economical meal in Essaouira.

**1:30pm – 3pm**: Go up to the Skala de la Ville (north ramparts with cannons). Views over the Atlantic. Walk along the exterior promenade below the ramparts.

**3pm – 4:30pm**: Explore the souks: thuya zone (boxes, chess sets), spice souk, craftsmen's alleyways.

**4:30pm – 5:30pm**: Coffee or tea in Plaza Moulay Hassan. Rest and atmosphere.

**5:30pm – 6pm**: Back to bus terminal. Return bus to Marrakech (6pm or 6:30pm).

Arrival in Marrakech around 9:30pm-10pm.

## If you stay overnight: what to add on day 2

If you decide to stay one night, day 2 opens up far more interesting options:

**Night 1**: Walk the medina at night (without day tourists), dinner at a medina restaurant, listen to Gnawa music in the square.

**Day 2 morning**: Go to the beach or Sidi Kaouki, port market early, visit the regional museum, thuya workshops in the morning.

**Day 2 afternoon**: Return to Marrakech at 2pm-3pm — you arrive in time for dinner at Jemaa el-Fna.

## One-night accommodation in Essaouira

For one night, riads inside the medina are the best option: the experience is complete, you're in the centre of everything, and prices are reasonable outside high season.

**Price ranges**:
- Simple guesthouses: 150-300 MAD (14-28€/night)
- Mid-range riads: 400-700 MAD (37-65€/night)
- Luxury riads: 1000-2500 MAD (90-230€/night)

Book ahead in July, August and during the Gnaoua Festival (June).
