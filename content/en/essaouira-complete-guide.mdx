Essaouira is one of those cities that catches you off guard. The first time you walk through the medina gate and the Atlantic wind hits your face, you understand why locals call it the **city of trade winds**. This guide covers everything you need to plan it well, whether as a day trip from Marrakech or as a destination in its own right.

## Why visit Essaouira

Essaouira doesn't compete with Marrakech in intensity or with Fès in historical depth. Its value is different: it's a fortified coastal city with a **UNESCO-listed medina**, endless white sand beaches, and a more relaxed atmosphere than any other city in Morocco. It's also the Moroccan capital of Gnawa music and one of the world's best kitesurfing spots.

If you're coming from inland cities — Marrakech, the imperial cities, the desert — Essaouira is a perfect contrast: sea air, different light, slower pace.

## How to get to Essaouira

### From Marrakech

This is the most common route. Three options:

- **CTM or Supratours bus**: frequent departures from Marrakech terminal. Journey of 3 hours 30 minutes. Price ~80-120 MAD (7-11€). The most economical option.
- **Shared grand taxi**: from Bab Doukkala taxi station. More flexible on timing, but you wait for it to fill up (6 passengers). Similar price to the bus.
- **Private taxi or rental car**: 3 hours on good road (N1 to Chichaoua, then P2221). With your own car, more freedom to stop along the way.

### From Agadir

Only 170 km to the south along the N1. About 2 hours 30 minutes by car. CTM also runs several daily buses.

### From Casablanca

About 350 km, just over 4 hours by car. CTM operates multiple daily departures.

## What to see in Essaouira

### The medina and ramparts

The heart of Essaouira is its **walled medina**, built in the 18th century by French architect Théodore Cornut under the orders of Sultan Sidi Mohammed ben Abdallah. The mix of European urban planning and Moroccan architecture gives it a unique character.

The **Skala de la Ville** (sea ramparts) is the most photogenic spot: historical bronze cannons overlooking the Atlantic. You have to go up. It's free.

The **Skala du Port** is next to the fishing port and offers another perspective on the ramparts and blue boats.

### The port and fish auction

Get to the port before 11am and you'll see the fish auction: tuna, sardines, prawns freshly unloaded from boats. The atmosphere is authentic. Right next to the port there are stalls where they cook to order the fish you just watched come off the water — eating seafood here is one of the best gastronomic moments in Morocco.

### The souks

Essaouira's souks are calmer than Marrakech's. Less pressure, more space to browse. The **thuya wood souk** stands out: Essaouira is the only place in the world where thuya is commercially worked. The objects made of thuya (boxes, chess sets, frames) are the quintessential local souvenir.

### The beaches

The main beach starts just south of the medina and extends several kilometres. In summer the wind can be very strong (up to 35 knots), which makes it perfect for kitesurfing but more challenging for sunbathing. In spring and autumn the wind is more moderate.

**Sidi Kaouki**, 30 minutes south by the coast road, has a quieter beach and is the epicentre of kitesurf schools.

## Where to eat in Essaouira

**Port stalls**: the cheapest and most authentic option. Pick your fish at one stall, have it cooked at the next. Sardines, prawns, squid.

**Les Alizés** (medina): quality Moroccan cuisine. Book ahead in high season.

**Taros Café** (terrace overlooking Plaza Moulay Hassan): ideal for tea with views. The café is decent, the views are excellent.

## Where to stay

**Riads in the medina**: the most recommended experience. Book in advance in high season (July-August is peak).

**Guesthouses and hostels**: good budget options from 15€/night.

## When to go to Essaouira

Essaouira has an Atlantic climate that makes it pleasant almost year-round.

- **Spring (March-May)**: the best season. Temperatures of 18-24°C, moderate wind, spectacular light.
- **Summer (June-August)**: most visited. Very strong wind (ideal for kite, difficult for beach). The Gnawa Festival in June fills the city.
- **Autumn (September-November)**: second best season. Fewer tourists, good temperature.
- **Winter (December-February)**: quiet. Can be rainy but sunny days exist.

## How much time do you need?

- **One day (day trip)**: enough to see the medina, ramparts and port.
- **2 days**: the optimum if you want to see everything comfortably and enjoy the evening.
- **3-4 days**: if you want to kitesurf, visit Sidi Kaouki or simply rest.

## Practical tips

- Bring a jacket or light layer: the wind lowers the feels-like temperature even in summer.
- Parking outside the ramparts is the only option if you go by car — the medina has no vehicle access.
- ATMs are available in the new town, just outside the medina walls.
- Haggling: yes in the souks. No in restaurants with written prices.
