The Moroccan Atlas is the highest mountain range in North Africa and one of the best-kept secrets in Moroccan tourism. Less than an hour from Marrakech, it offers a radical contrast to the city: silence, cool air, red Berber villages and landscapes ranging from the deep green of valleys to the permanent white of summits.

This guide covers everything you need to know to visit the Atlas: what to see, how to get there, how much time to spend and what expectations to bring.

---

## Why the Atlas surprises visitors

Most travelers going to Morocco have the desert as their main destination. The Atlas, by contrast, is still relatively unknown outside of specialist trekking circuits. This has a practical advantage: uncrowded trails, villages where tourists are still a novelty, and a genuinely different experience from anything you've seen in Europe.

The High Atlas has peaks over 4,000 meters. Jebel Toubkal (4,167 m) is the highest peak in all of North Africa. The climb requires no mountaineering experience in summer.

## The main zones of the Moroccan Atlas

### The central High Atlas (Toubkal and Imlil)

The most visited zone and also the most spectacular in terms of altitude. The village of Imlil (1,740 m) is the starting point for the ascent of Toubkal and for multi-day trekking routes through Berber valleys.

**What to expect:** Alpine landscape, local guides, good infrastructure for mountain hikers. In summer it's green and warm during the day; in winter there is deep snow.

**How to get there:** Grand taxi from Asni (47 km from Marrakech) to Imlil. The complete journey from Marrakech by private taxi is about 75 km.

### The Ourika Valley

The most accessible valley from Marrakech (65 km). The Ourika river descends from the Toubkal snows through a green valley with a weekly market at Tnine-Ourika and waterfalls at the end of the route in Setti Fatma.

Ideal for a day trip with no special physical preparation.

### The Dadès and Todra Gorges

Located on the southern edge of the High Atlas, these gorges are two of Morocco's most spectacular landscapes. The Todra Gorge has vertical walls 300 meters high. The Dadès has a road that climbs in tight bends known as the "Thousand Kasbahs."

Both are on the natural route between Marrakech and the desert.

### The Agafay Desert

Technically not Atlas but its southern extension: a rocky plateau 30 km from Marrakech with a minimalist aesthetic very different from the sand Sahara. No mass tourism, no dunes, no obligatory camels. Perfect for a glamping night.

## Trekking: what level is needed

The Atlas has routes for all levels:

| Level | Example route | Duration |
|---|---|---|
| Easy | Setti Fatma waterfalls (Ourika) | 1h ascent |
| Moderate | Toubkal refuge from Imlil | 1 day (5-6h) |
| Difficult | Toubkal summit | 2 days + acclimatization |
| Expert | M'Goun traverse (4,071 m) | 5-7 days |

For Toubkal in summer, good physical fitness and mountain boots are sufficient. No technical equipment needed between July and September.

## When to visit the Atlas

The ideal season varies by activity:

- **Spring (March-May):** Best for trekking and valleys. Almond and cherry trees blossom in the valleys. Toubkal may have snow on the summit but is generally accessible.
- **Autumn (September-November):** Second best option. Clear air, fewer tourists than in summer.
- **Summer (June-August):** Valleys are pleasant; Toubkal summit is in perfect condition. Heat at lower altitudes.
- **Winter (December-February):** Deep snow. Toubkal requires snow gear. Lower valleys are cold but often clear.

## How to combine the Atlas with Marrakech

The Atlas can be integrated into almost any trip to Marrakech:

**Option 1 — Day trip (Ourika Valley or Agafay):** Leave in the morning, return in the afternoon. Nothing special needed.

**Option 2 — 2 days (Imlil + Toubkal):** Day 1 to Imlil, trek to the refuge; Day 2 summit + return to Marrakech.

**Option 3 — 4-5 days (Atlas + Gorges + Desert):** The classic south route: Marrakech → Tichka Pass → Dadès Gorges → Todra Gorges → Merzouga Desert.

## Accommodation in the Atlas

- **Imlil:** Berber guesthouses with breakfast included (100-250 MAD/night). Wide range from basic to comfortable.
- **Toubkal Refuge (CAF):** Dormitories at 3,207 m. Open year-round. Advance booking needed in peak season.
- **Ourika Valley:** Some riads and kasbahs, mainly in the lower valley.
- **Agafay:** Glamping and luxury lodges. The most expensive and most photogenic option.
