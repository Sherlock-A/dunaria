Marrakech to Fes is 570 km as the crow flies and several ways to make the journey. The fastest is the train (7h15, non-stop). The most interesting is the road route, which crosses the Atlas via the Tichka pass, descends toward the Dadès Valley, climbs to the Middle Atlas and arrives in Fes from the south.

This guide covers the road route with 2-3 days to spare — the most recommended approach if you have the time for it.

---

## The two options

### Option 1 — Train (direct route)
**Duration:** 7h15 (direct), 8h30 with Meknès stop.
**Price:** 180-220 MAD second class.
**Best for:** Travelers with limited time who want to arrive in Fes rested.

### Option 2 — Road (rental car or private circuit)
**Duration:** 2-3 days with stops.
**Actual distance:** 560-650 km depending on route chosen.
**Best for:** Travelers with 7+ days in Morocco who want to see the gorges and Atlas.

---

## The road route: essential stops

### Day 1: Marrakech → Aït Ben Haddou (200 km)

**Col du Tichka (2,260 m):**
The highest mountain pass in the Moroccan Atlas. The road climbs in switchbacks between 3,000m peaks. Mandatory stop at the highest point for the views.

**Aït Ben Haddou (30 km before Ouarzazate):**
Morocco's most famous ksar (adobe fortress city). UNESCO World Heritage. Film set (Gladiator, Game of Thrones). Cross the river on foot or by boat to enter. Climb to the upper section for complete ksar views.

**Sleep in:** Ouarzazate or at the Aït Ben Haddou ksar.

---

### Day 2: Aït Ben Haddou → Dadès Gorges (260 km)

**Ouarzazate:**
The "Gateway to the Desert" has its own craft market and Atlas Studios film sets (one of the world's largest). 2h visit is sufficient.

**Drâa Valley:**
Between Ouarzazate and Zagora, a 150 km palm grove following the Drâa river. The palm groves and adobe kasbahs are the most characteristic landscape of the deep south.

**Dadès Gorges:**
One of Morocco's most photogenic canyons. The road climbs in tight bends between red rock walls.

**Sleep in:** Boumalne Dadès, inside the canyon.

---

### Day 3: Dadès → Midelt → Fes (350 km)

**Todra Gorges:**
20 km from the Dadès, Morocco's other great canyon. 300m high walls, 10m wide at the narrowest point. A place that impresses regardless of how many photos you've seen.

**Midelt and the Middle Atlas:**
Climbing from the south, the road crosses the Middle Atlas — cedar forests, Berber villages and a temperature that drops 15 degrees compared to the south.

**Arrival in Fes:**
Late afternoon or evening. The drive from Midelt is 3h30.

| Factor | Train | Road |

| Cost | ~220 MAD/person | 400-600 MAD/day (rental) |
| Experience | Comfortable and practical | Enriching, more tiring |
