Argan oil is one of the best-selling souvenirs in Morocco. It's also one of the most faked. This guide explains how to tell quality argan oil from the rest, where to buy it in the Essaouira area — the heart of the production zone — and what price to expect.

## What argan is and why it's special

The **argan** tree (*Argania spinosa*) grows only in the **Essaouira-Agadir-Taroudant triangle** in southwest Morocco. This region is the only place in the world where it grows commercially. UNESCO declared it a **Biosphere Reserve** in 1998.

The tree produces fruits from which oil is extracted. The process is labour-intensive: the fruits are dried, pulped, the nuts are cracked by hand, the seeds are roasted or used raw, and cold-pressed. To produce one litre of oil you need about 30 kg of fruit — and roughly 15 hours of manual work.

The result is an oil with exceptional nutritional and cosmetic properties:
- High **oleic acid** content (similar to olive oil)
- High **vitamin E** content (three times that of olive oil)
- Tocopherols and sterols that make it very stable

## Types of argan oil

### Culinary argan oil

Made from **roasted** seeds. The toasting gives it that characteristic **nutty flavour** — similar to walnut or toasted sesame, but milder.

Used for:
- Dipping bread at breakfast (with amlou, the almond and honey paste)
- Dressing salads and vegetables
- Finishing meat or fish dishes with a drizzle
- NOT used for high-temperature cooking (loses its properties)

### Cosmetic argan oil

Made from **raw, unroasted** seeds. The extraction process is similar but more delicate. The result is an odourless (or very faintly scented) oil, paler yellow in colour.

Used for:
- Face and body moisturising
- Treatment of dry or damaged hair
- Anti-ageing (high vitamin E concentration)
- Base for creams and cosmetics

### Amlou

Not oil — it's a paste of **ground almonds + culinary argan oil + honey**. It's the traditional breakfast of the region. Spread on fresh bread. Delicious and very calorie-dense.

## How to recognise genuine argan oil

### Signs of quality

**Colour**: culinary oil should be **light golden-yellow**. Cosmetic: very pale yellow, almost colourless. Very dark or very bright oil should raise suspicion.

**Scent**: culinary oil smells of toasted nuts. Cosmetic has a faint, vegetal aroma. If the oil has no smell at all or smells like sunflower oil, it's probably adulterated.

**Consistency**: pure oil is more viscous than olive oil but less than coconut oil. If it's very fluid it may be blended.

**Origin on label**: look for "huile d'argan 100% pure" or "pure argan oil" with indication of origin "Maroc" and the co-operative's seal.

### Signs of fraud

- Price too low: quality argan oil cannot be cheap (read the prices section below)
- Seller doing a "foam demonstration" by rubbing oil in their hands — this proves nothing
- Bottle without detailed label or with generic label
- Very dark or very light (whitish) colour
- Souk vendors who take you to their "factory" on the way generally charge double compared to co-operatives

## Where to buy well in the Essaouira area

### Women's co-operatives

The best option to guarantee quality and fair price is to buy directly from a **women's producer co-operative**. The co-operative employs women from rural communities who produce the oil by hand and receive a fair wage.

Several recognised co-operatives are on the road between Essaouira and Agadir and between Essaouira and Marrakech:

**Tighanimine Co-operative**: on the Essaouira-Agadir road, km 20. One of the best known with a bus stop. You can see the extraction process, try the amlou and buy directly.

**Aknari Co-operative**: in Essaouira city. Shop in the medina with fixed prices and certificate of origin.

### Certified shops

Look for shops displaying the **DEFR seal** (Moroccan denomination of origin) or certification from the **Fédération Nationale des Coopératives d'Argane**. These seals guarantee 100% Moroccan origin and artisanal process.

## Approximate prices (2025)

| Channel | Culinary litre | Cosmetic litre |
|---|---|---|
| Direct from co-operative | 150-250 MAD (14-23€) | 200-350 MAD (18-32€) |
| Medina shop | 200-350 MAD | 300-500 MAD |
| Airport or tourist city | 400-800 MAD | 500-1000 MAD |

Prices at the airport and in Marrakech or Fès shops are notably higher than buying directly in the production area.

## How to use argan oil

### Culinary

- Moroccan breakfast: amlou (almond + honey + argan) with bread
- Salad: a few drops as a final touch
- Couscous or tagine: a few drops raw at the end of cooking
- As an appetiser with flatbread

### Cosmetic

- Face: 2-3 drops in the palms, warm and apply to dry face before sleeping
- Hair: 2-3 drops on wet hair ends before drying
- Body: mixed with regular moisturising cream
- Cuticles: one drop on each dry nail
