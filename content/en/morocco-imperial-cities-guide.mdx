Morocco has four imperial cities: Fes, Marrakech, Meknès and Rabat. Each served at some point as the country's capital, and each has preserved something of the historical grandeur that sets it apart from any other city in the Maghreb.

This is the complete map for understanding what each offers and how to plan an itinerary combining them.

---

## What are the imperial cities?

The term "imperial city" refers to the four cities that served as capital of the Moroccan sultanate over the centuries:

- **Fes** (founded 789, capital during 12th-20th centuries)
- **Marrakech** (founded 1070, capital during three different periods)
- **Meknès** (brief but intense capital under Moulay Ismail, 17th century)
- **Rabat** (capital since 1912, current government seat)

This isn't a tourist invention: these four cities concentrate Morocco's densest architecture, craftsmanship and history.

## The four cities: quick comparison

| City | Main appeal | Minimum time | Distance between them |
|---|---|---|---|
| Fes | Most authentic medina in the Arab world | 2-3 days | — |
| Marrakech | Jemaa el Fna, souks, nightlife | 2-3 days | 6h from Fes |
| Meknès | Underrated heritage, no crowds | 1 day | 1h from Fes |
| Rabat | Modern capital + historic medina | 1 day | 3h from Fes |

---

## Fes: the intact medieval city

Fes is the most complex city and the most rewarding for those who take time. Unlike Marrakech, where the medina has evolved into a sophisticated tourist hub, Fes feels real: artisans work as they did six centuries ago, neighborhoods function as independent villages, and getting lost in its 9,000 alleys is not a metaphor — it's literally possible.

**Must-sees:**
- Fès el-Bali medina (UNESCO World Heritage)
- Bou Inania and Al-Attarine medersas
- Chouara tanneries (access from leather shop terraces)
- Al-Andalus quarter
- Mellah (historic Jewish quarter)

**Time needed:** 2 days minimum, 3 to do it properly.

---

## Marrakech: the most cosmopolitan

Marrakech is most European travelers' entry point to Morocco and the country's tourism hub. Its medina is more visitor-oriented than Fes's, but remains authentic on its fringes and in the early mornings.

**Must-sees:**
- Jemaa el Fna (the square that never stops)
- Medina souks
- Bahia Palace and the Saadian Tombs
- Majorelle Garden
- Mellah quarter (the most overlooked by mass tourism)

---

## Meknès: the forgotten imperial city

Meknès has something the other three cities don't: silence. It was the capital of Moulay Ismail (1672-1727), the builder sultan who wanted to make Morocco the Versailles of North Africa. His ambition left behind impressive ruins that very few people visit.

**Must-sees:**
- Bab Mansour (Morocco's largest monumental gate)
- Moulay Ismail Mausoleum
- Medina and Bou Inania Medersa
- Volubilis Roman ruins (30 km away)

**Tip:** Meknès combines perfectly with Fes in one to one and a half days.

---

## Rabat: the quiet capital

Rabat is the easiest imperial city to visit: well-organized, less vendor pressure, well-connected by train. Its medina is small but well-preserved, and the Oudayas Kasbah is one of the most photogenic spaces in the country.

**Must-sees:**
- Oudayas Kasbah and its gardens
- Hassan Tower and Mohammed V Mausoleum
- Medina and Chellah (Moorish-Roman necropolis)

## Recommended itineraries

**5 days (minimum circuit):**
Casablanca (arrival) → Rabat (1 day) → Meknès (1 day) → Fes (2 days) → flight from Fes or bus to Marrakech

**7 days (comfortable circuit):**
Marrakech (2 days) → Meknès (1 day) → Fes (2 days) → Chefchaouen (2 days) → flight from Fes or Tangier

**10 days (with the south):**
Marrakech → imperial cities → Fes → Chefchaouen → Tangier + option of adding 2-3 desert days in the south.
