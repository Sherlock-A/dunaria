Essaouira is pleasant year-round — the Atlantic regulates the temperature and the city never reaches the heat extremes of Morocco's interior. But depending on what you want to do, some months are clearly better than others. This guide explains it month by month.

## Essaouira's climate in brief

Temperature in Essaouira ranges between **10-15°C in winter** and **22-28°C in summer**. But the element that defines the Essaouira experience isn't temperature: it's the **wind**.

The **Atlantic trade wind** (called *alisio* or *trade wind* in Morocco) blows from the northwest between June and August, sometimes at 25-35 knots of force. This wind keeps the city cool in summer but makes the beach uncomfortable if you don't like sand flying.

Rainfall is scarce: the area receives about 350mm annually, concentrated in winter and spring.

## Month by month: what to expect

### January and February

**Temperature**: 10-16°C. Moderate wind. Can be rainy.

Essaouira in winter is quiet and photogenic. Medina streets are almost empty of tourists, craftsmen work without interruption, and accommodation prices are the year's lowest.

Not beach or kitesurfing season. But if what you want is a real city without layers of tourism, January-February is worth considering.

**Ideal for**: independent travellers wanting authenticity and low cost.

### March and April

**Temperature**: 14-20°C. Moderate wind, sunny days.

Spring comes to Essaouira earlier than to the interior. Afternoons are pleasant, wind is mild (15-20 knots on windier days), and the sea starts to be swimmable for the brave (water temperature: 17-18°C).

The countryside around Essaouira is green after winter rains — the argan landscape in bloom is particularly beautiful.

**Ideal for**: cultural tourism, gastronomy, first kite sessions for beginners.

### May and June

**Temperature**: 18-24°C. Wind increasing towards June.

May is possibly the best month of the year to visit Essaouira. Weather is perfect, the city isn't yet crowded, and the wind is consistent enough for kitesurfing without being aggressive.

**June** is the month of the **Gnaoua Festival** (normally the last weekend). If you want to see it, organise months in advance for accommodation: Essaouira fills completely.

**Ideal for**: all types of traveller. The best balance of weather, wind (kite), cultural tourism and price.

### July and August

**Temperature**: 22-27°C. Strong wind (25-35 knots for most of the day).

Essaouira's summer is different from Morocco's interior summer. No oppressive heat — the wind prevents it. But that same wind makes the beach uncomfortable for sunbathing or swimming if you don't like flying sand.

Instead, it's the golden season for **kitesurfing and windsurfing** — the world's best riders come in July-August. The camps in Sidi Kaouki are at maximum capacity.

Tourism is at its peak: medina full of visitors, restaurants with waiting times, accommodation prices at their highest.

**Ideal for**: kitesurfers and windsurfers. Not ideal for families with small children or those wanting a quiet beach.

### September and October

**Temperature**: 20-25°C. Wind gradually dropping.

One of the best seasons. Temperatures are perfect, wind eases (especially October), the sea has warmed up during summer (water at 20-22°C) and mass tourists have gone home.

The autumn light in Essaouira, with the low sun on the white ramparts, is beautiful.

**Ideal for**: beach, cultural tourism, gastronomy, solo and couple travellers.

### November and December

**Temperature**: 12-18°C. Variable wind, can be rainy.

Low season. The city returns to its local rhythm. Craftsmen, fishermen, the everyday life of Essaouira are more visible than in summer.

The Atlantic in November produces large waves — good surfing but not for bathers. The Gnaoua Winter Festival Edition sometimes takes place in November with a smaller programme.

**Ideal for**: photographers, travellers wanting peace and quiet, wave surfing.

## Summary: best time by what you want

| If you want... | Best month(s) |
|---|---|
| Beach and swimming | September-October, May |
| Advanced kitesurfing | July-August |
| Beginner kitesurfing | September, May |
| Gnaoua Festival | June (last weekend) |
| Without mass tourists | January-February, November |
| Best weather overall | May, September-October |
| Lowest price | January-February |

## Water temperature by month

| Month | Water temperature |
|---|---|
| January-March | 16-18°C |
| April-June | 17-19°C |
| July-August | 18-20°C |
| September-October | 20-22°C |
| November-December | 17-19°C |

The Moroccan Atlantic is cool year-round — don't expect the Mediterranean in summer. Locals don't swim much. Northern European tourists find it perfectly acceptable.
