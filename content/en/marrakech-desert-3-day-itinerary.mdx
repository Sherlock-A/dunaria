Three days is the minimum to do Marrakech–Merzouga–Marrakech justice. It's fast, but if you move efficiently, you'll see the best of the southern Morocco circuit: the ksar of Aït Ben Haddou, the Dadès and Todra gorges, and a night in the Sahara dunes. This is the route, stop by stop.

## Overview

- **Distance**: approximately 1,100 km total (round trip)
- **Driving**: roughly 16–18 hours across three days
- **Best for**: 3–4 people in a rental car, or booked as a private tour
- **Minimum budget**: €200–250/person (budget transport and basic camp)
- **Comfortable budget**: €400–600/person (private car + mid-range accommodation)

---

## Day 1: Marrakech → Ouarzazate → Aït Ben Haddou

**Driving**: ~250 km, 3.5–4 hours not counting stops

**Leave early** — ideally by 7:30–8:00am. The road south crosses the High Atlas via the **Tizi n'Tichka pass** (2,260 m). This section alone is worth the trip: a series of switchbacks through red and ochre rock, occasional Berber villages clinging to hillsides, and views that have appeared in countless films.

### Aït Ben Haddou (stop: 1.5–2 hours)
The UNESCO-listed ksar (fortified village) is 30 km before Ouarzazate. You've seen it in *Gladiator*, *Lawrence of Arabia*, *Game of Thrones*, and dozens of others without knowing it. Walk through the lower village, climb to the kasbah at the top for panoramic views over the river valley, and take the obligatory photo from the far bank.

**Entry**: 10 MAD (about €1).

### Ouarzazate (lunch stop or overnight)
The "gateway to the desert" is a functional mid-size town worth a 1–2 hour stop: the Atlas Corporation Studios (filming location for many Hollywood productions), the Taourirt Kasbah in the town centre. If you want to break the trip and sleep comfortably before the long drive to Merzouga, Ouarzazate has the best hotel options on the route (€60–120 for a decent riad).

**If continuing**: From Ouarzazate, it's another 4–5 hours to Merzouga — possible to do in one afternoon, but tiring. Most people split it.

**Sleep**: Riad or hotel in Ouarzazate (~€40–80/room) or push on toward the gorges.

## Day 2: Ouarzazate → Gorges → Merzouga

**Driving**: ~350 km, 5–6 hours with stops

### Dadès Gorge (1–1.5 hours)
About 2 hours east of Ouarzazate. The road enters a narrow canyon of eroded red rock. The most photographed section is the **Monkey Fingers** (or Chicken Claws) — a group of eroded rock formations about 27 km into the gorge. Stop, walk around, take photos, get back on the road.

### Todra Gorge (1 hour)
20 minutes from Tinghir, the Todra is a 300-metre-high slot canyon through which a river runs. At its narrowest point, the walls are only about 10 metres apart. Visitors walk through the gorge floor — there are cafes on both sides. Better than it sounds, even on a rushed itinerary.

### Erfoud and Rissani
Two market towns before Merzouga. Fill up on petrol in Erfoud — the last reliable station before the desert. Rissani has a large Berber market on Tuesdays, Thursdays, and Sundays.

### Merzouga arrival (late afternoon)
Aim to arrive by 4–5pm to leave time for the camel ride at sunset. The standard sunset camel trek from most camps takes 45–60 minutes; you ride out to a high dune, watch the sun go down, and either walk back or ride again in the dark.

**Sleep**: Desert camp (€25–100/person depending on category). Book ahead in spring and autumn.

## Day 3: Merzouga → Sunrise → Marrakech

**Driving**: ~550 km, 7–9 hours

### Sunrise in the dunes (5:00–6:30am)
Wake up early — your camp will have an alarm call service. Walking to a high dune in the dark, watching the sky go from black to purple to orange to gold over the Erg Chebbi: this is the moment people remember.

Photography tip: the first 15 minutes after sunrise, when the light is low and the shadows are long, are the best. The colors saturate fast.

### Return route options

**Option A: Same road back** — fastest (8.5 hours direct), but you've already seen it.

**Option B: Northern route via Midelt** — slightly longer but passes through a different landscape (cedar forests of Azrou if you detour slightly). About 9 hours.

**Option C: Extend by one day** — by far the best choice if you can manage it. Sleep in Ouarzazate or Aït Ben Haddou on the way back and arrive in Marrakech rested on Day 4 morning.

### Arrival in Marrakech
Budget 8–9 hours for the drive. Plan to arrive no later than 7–8pm.

## Tips for this route

- **Avoid driving the High Atlas at night**: the Tizi n'Tichka pass is fine in daylight but stressful in darkness; plan your timing accordingly
- **Book the camp before the gorges**: don't leave it to chance in high season
- **The gorges are not optional**: both Dadès and Todra take less than 2 hours each and are among the best landscapes in Morocco
- **Take the long way at least once**: the return via the north (Midelt–Azrou–Ifrane) adds variety
